The solar system consists of the Sun and everything that orbits it, including eight planets,
their moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, and comets. The eight planets in order from the Sun are
Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Mercury is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun. Venus is the hottest planet due to a
thick, heat-trapping atmosphere. Earth is the only known planet with life. Mars is called the
Red Planet because of iron oxide on its surface. Jupiter is the largest planet, a gas giant with
a famous storm called the Great Red Spot. Saturn is known for its extensive ring system made of
ice and rock. Uranus rotates on its side, giving it extreme seasons. Neptune is the farthest
planet and has the strongest winds in the solar system.

The Sun itself is a star, a giant ball of hot plasma that generates energy through nuclear
fusion, converting hydrogen into helium at its core. This fusion process is what produces the
light and heat that sustains life on Earth.
