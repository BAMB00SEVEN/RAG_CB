Building healthy daily habits has a large impact on long-term wellbeing. Regular physical
activity, such as at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week, strengthens the heart,
improves mood, and helps maintain a healthy body weight. Strength training exercises performed
two or more days a week help build and preserve muscle mass.

Good sleep hygiene is equally important. Adults generally need seven to nine hours of sleep per
night. Keeping a consistent sleep schedule, avoiding screens before bed, and keeping the bedroom
cool and dark can significantly improve sleep quality.

A balanced diet rich in vegetables, fruits, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats
supports energy levels and long-term health. Staying hydrated by drinking enough water throughout
the day is also important for concentration and physical performance.

Managing stress through techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, journaling, or spending
time outdoors can reduce the negative effects of chronic stress on both mental and physical
health.
